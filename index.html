BIAS IN ALGORITHMS

AI Inherits Our Biases

Bias can be seen as a human's instinct to make snap judgment's. When this element is integrated into AI the machine will reflect the programmer rather then reflecting a objective reality. Components of bias in AI include:

  • Failing to anticipate how machine learning might be manipulated or how it may interpret human data.
  • Malicious actors intentionally feeding toxic data.
  • Using data that lacks diversity.
  • Implicit bias of well intended engineers seeping into data sets and algorithms.
Institute, T. (2018). Can we protect AI from our biases? | Robin Hauser | TED Institute. In Youtube.

Algorithms Codify Past Inequities

AI Algorithms have largely been trained on data that has existed on the internet long before AI was created. The internet has contained historical biases, not just because historically marginalized groups have had less access to the internet but also because it is of human nature to be bias; those biases have now been embedded into training data that AI uses to train it's models, codifying the past. For this reason we must be careful when integrating AI into our society. For example, using AI in criminal justice can be problematic, think about the past arrest records, and how past prejudices may be automatically embed into the AI.

Wachter-Boettcher, S. (2017). TECHNICALLY WRONG : why digital products are designed to fail you. W W Norton.
